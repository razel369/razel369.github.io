Best AI Tools for Social Media Content (2026)

Last updated: July 2, 2026

If you run social for a small business, AI social tools made 2026's workflow completely different from 2023. The mature category is now: write-the-caption, design-the-image, schedule-the-post, all in one tool. The category is also saturated with half-broken tools. We tested the five that genuinely save time.

Quick recommendation

For a single platform focus (LinkedIn or X): Predis.ai. For multi-platform posting with AI captions: Brandwell. For an all-in-one that includes design: Canva Magic Studio + Buffer. For creators whose primary content is short-form video: skip these and use a video-first tool.

Predis.ai — the LinkedIn/X specialist

Predis is the strongest AI caption generator for LinkedIn posts and X threads. It pulls trending topics in your niche and drafts a week of posts in 5 minutes. The catch: it does not publish directly to LinkedIn on free tier. You still need a schedulable account. $29/mo.

Brandwell — the cross-platform all-rounder

Brandwell covers LinkedIn, X, Threads, Instagram in one composer. AI captions match each platform's tone (more clinical on LinkedIn, more punchy on X). $24/mo is fair for the time it saves.

Ocoya — the copy-led option

Ocoya is a copy-first social tool: AI captions + AI hashtag suggestions + AI image prompts. Less polished than Brandwell but the underlying AI feels like ChatGPT with social-media fine-tuning. $19/mo is the cheapest of the three multi-platform tools.

Buffer AI — add-on to a trusted name

Buffer is the default social scheduler for many small businesses. In 2026 they added an AI Assistant that generates post variants and rewrites based on top-performing past posts. It's a paid add-on on top of $6/channel. Reasonable; not as strong as Predis for LinkedIn.

Canva Magic Studio — the design-first choice

If your social work is mostly visual (quotes, carousels, image-driven posts), Canva's Magic Studio handles AI design + AI caption in one app. Magic Resize resizes a single design for every platform in 30 seconds. Magic Studio AI image generation is solid for backgrounds and pattern fills, weak for "I want a portrait of my customer" use cases. Try Canva

What we did not include

  • Lately AI: repurposes long-form content into social. Niche use case. Skipped.
  • Hootsuite OwlyWriter: OwlyWriter AI is ok but the broader Hootsuite product is enterprise-priced. Skip for SMBs.
  • ContentStudio: solid for agencies, cluttered for solopreneurs.

The practical stack

Most small businesses end up with one social tool + Canva for design. If posting volume is < 30 posts/week, Buffer + Canva covers it. If you publish 3+ posts/day, Brandwell or Ocoya replaces both and saves 5-10 hours/week.
